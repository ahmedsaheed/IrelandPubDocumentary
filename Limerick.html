Limerick


The city dates from 812, but history suggests the presence of earlier settlements in the area, as an important battle involving Cormac mac Airt in 221 and a visit by St. Patrick in 434. Limerick has a population of 105000 inhabitants and In 2014, Limerick became Ireland's inaugural National City of Culture, with a variety of artistic and cultural events occurring at locations around the city throughout the year.

Guide to the Pubs in Limerick

Among the experiences that you can have here, you will be able to taste their renowned whiskey beside a fireplace, all while listening to the many live music performances that many local pubs offer, traditional or not. With its high student population, Limerick enjoys a vibrant nightlife, catering to all tastes and ages.

Fitzgerald's Bar
 Great pub with lots of things to do along with entertainment every weekend, along with bingo nights,darts ,snooker, party nights for holidays. The food is really good and well able to hold fine crowd for large party's.

The Black Swan
 Established over 100 years ago it has warm welcoming atmosphere, helpful staff and happy mix of customers. Is the perfect place to come to watch the match, listen to live music or enjoy time with friends tucked away in the Snug.

Costello's Tavern
 Is a beautiful two floors, late bar, indie and rock music and beer pong, it has open fireplace and is situated just a 2 minute walk from limericks main nightclub and bar area by the Milk Market.

Katie Dalys Heritage
 Katies Dalys in the heart of the Limerick’s medieval quarter, A stone throw away from King John’s Castle. Its a perfect place for local drinks, great tasting food and warm atmosphere.

The Locke Bar
 Just beside the river, with a huge outdoor seating and entertainment area, brings a unique offering to the city of Limerick.
With a history that started in the 1700's, it's one of the oldest pubs in the city. For seven nights a week, you can enjoy The Big Limerick Seisiún, a Traditional Irish Music & Dance Session!
