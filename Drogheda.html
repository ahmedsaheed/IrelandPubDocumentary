Drogheda


Drogheda means "bridge at the ford". Is one of oldest towns in Ireland, with a population of 41000 people.
The town is situated in an area which contains a number of archaeological monuments dating from the Neolithic period. It is a place that deserves to be seen!

Guide to the Pubs in Drogheda

Drogheda nightlife is a hidden gem, with over 50 pubs, two nightclubs and dozens of restaurants there is always something to do.

The Punt
 The Punt Pub, one of the oldest public houses in Drogheda has kept its doors open right back to prior the Civil War in 1922. The pub was also finalist for Drogheda Business excellence Awards in 2018.

McHug's
McHugh’s Pub has been a long-standing popular choice for both locals and tourists alike.
It brings many live acts to Drogheda and with the addition of The Venue, is now one of the town’s top live music venues.

Clarke's Bar
 Is a traditional and unique snug bar with regular live music and great staff, dating back to 1900 and it remained pretty much the same since then. The pint of Guinness is the main tipple and its said that no better pint can be had for miles around.

Gleesons
 One of Drogheda towns oldest and most loved pub. You will receive a warm welcome and will be able to enjoy live sport. There different events and festival that keeps the pub always a great place to be.

Millmount Bar
 Millmount is one of Droghedas oldest pub and it takes its name from the nearby Millmount fort on the man-made mound that dates back over 3,000 years ago.
Traditional and very friendly bar. You have to stop by.
