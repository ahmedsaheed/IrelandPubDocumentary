Waterford


It is the oldest and the fifth most populous city in the Republic of Ireland, with 53,504 people.
Today, Waterford is known for Waterford Crystal, a legacy of the city's former glassmaking industry.
Once a year the city hosts the Waterford Walls Festival, an international street art event where you can enjoy a day walking around the city and enjoying to see amazing graffiti along the walls of the city.

Guide to the Pubs in Waterford

Waterford is a characterful city that offers both visitors and locals a unique experience different from what you’ll find in the rest of Ireland. Vikings first founded this ancient city in 914 A.D, and what remains today is a major townland rich in local culture, heritage sites, and medieval infrastructure.

The Cove Bar
 Situated in Waterford City Centre, a Friendly and welcoming bar with a warm décor and traditional feel. A great place for chill with friends and family, with entertaining quizzes for fund raising propose, screens for watch sports match and live music.

The Three Shippes & The Brig Bar
 One of Waterford’s favourite locals, Classic and traditional Irish pub, with a nautical design, a place for watch sports match with a good selection of drinks and a friendly staff. Breakfast, lunch, and dinner available.

The Lady Belle
 A famous ship in Waterford gives inspiration for the Pubs name. In the mid-afternoon is a great place for a quiet drink and chat. Every Thursday and Friday live music. Serves breakfast, lunch, dinner, and homemade dessert.
In 2019 was chosen as pub of the year in the County.

T&H Doolans
 This is one of Irelands oldest pub(over 300 years old) with an authentic Irish atmosphere, famous world wide for its tradition, as Irish music sessions bringing some of the best musicians in the area, including Sinead O’Connor who began her career there and The Clancy Brothers who were formers owners

The Munster Bar
 Possibly Waterford’s oldest pub, the site has served as tavern since 1853. Situated in a great location, just a few steps from important attractions in the County. The Munster bar is also famous for the bacon ribs, a traditional meal served in the restaurant.
