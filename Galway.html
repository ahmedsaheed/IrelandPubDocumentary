Galway


Is the sixth most populous city in Ireland, with a population of 79,934. The city was created in the 1100's by King of Connacht and is often referred to as the “Cultural Heart of Ireland” as it is most associated with the Irish language, music, song and dance traditions. The coast has one of the most beautiful roads to travel, with amazing views.

Guide to the Pubs in Galway

The pubs in Galway are busy every night and you'll find traditional music sessions happening in various pubs around the place. The city has plenty of tourists, students and locals to make the experience unforgettable, you will not miss fun in Galway.

John Keogh's Gastropub
 High quality food including Irish steak, fresh West coast seafood and homemade pasta are only one of the details that made John Keogh’s a renowned and multiple award - winning gastropub in Galway. A place where you can celebrate from birthday parties to wedding after parties.

The Porterhouse
 Live music, freshest local produce used in the food preparation, a good selection of drinks and beers, private rooms, late bar, group discount and more for guarantee a memorable celebration. Breakfast, lunch and dinner available.

Taaffes Bar
 Located in the heart of Galway City, the Taaffes is one of the best knowns bar, operating for over 150 years. It is best known for its Traditional Irish music and some of Irelands best traditional musicians including Sharon Shannon has played there. There is plenty of seating along the walls and its busy seven nights a week.

The Quays
 Live Music Venue of the Year 2017 & Best Music Pub 2018 & 2019 Located in the city centre of Galway, The Quays is one of the most famous pubs, with more than 400 years. It is a must stop if you are in Galway.

Sonny Molloy's
 Award Winning Irish Whiskey Bar The building was part of a Medieval property built in the 1600’s and you can enjoy a masterclass with interactive tasting session that’s both fun and informative. For who likes Whiskey, this is definitely the place.
